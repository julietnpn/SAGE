SAgE - Software for Agricultural Ecosystems

SAGE (Software for Agricultural Ecosystems) is a collection of software applications that supports the public pursuit of sustainable agriculture, in cooperation with the Southern California sustainable agriculture community.

The SAGE Plant Database is a database of plants used for grassroots or amateur agroecology design, including ethnobotanical, cultivation, morphological, and phenological data.

The SAGE Composer is be a design tool for creating sustainable polycultures (i.e., assemblages of complementary and mutually beneficial plant species, typically composed primarily of perennials) for personal use.

This research is funded by the NSF under Grant #1442749 CyberSEES: Type 1: Fostering Non-Expert Creation of Sustainable Polycultures through Crowdsourced Data Synthesis.

This is the dissertation project of Juliet Norton. It is a collaborative effort between Juliet Norton and Bill Tomlinson at the University of California, Irvine, Don Patterson at Westmont College, and Sarah Lovell at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champagne.

For questions and details, contact Juliet at perennialpolycultures@ics.uci.edu
